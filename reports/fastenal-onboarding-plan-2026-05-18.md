# Fastenal Onboarding Plan - Glue Masters

Generated: 2026-05-18

## Objective

Get Glue Masters positioned for Fastenal supplier onboarding and first purchase order, without wasting cycles on a blind portal submission that has no store/category pull.

## What The Fastenal Docs Say

Official supplier portal path:
- New/potential suppliers register through fastenal.com via My Account / Sign In / Register.
- Registration must be completed by the supplier.
- New suppliers select Fastenal Supplier, then select that they are not an existing Fastenal supplier.
- If working with a Fastenal store, include the store's 5-letter ID; valid store codes are accepted and prioritized.
- If there is no current Fastenal relationship, leave the store field blank and continue.
- The request goes to Fastenal Supplier Compliance for review.
- Fastenal's FAQ says approval/denial notice is expected in about an hour after registration; if approved, Fastenal emails a Supplier ID.

Important contacts from official docs:
- Supplier registration/help: suppliercompliance@fastenal.com
- Supplier portal/current supplier help: supplierportal@fastenal.com
- EDI / supplier SOP: suppliersop@fastenal.com
- Product compliance / hazmat / COO: productcompliance@fastenal.com
- Hazmat class issues: hazmatissues@fastenal.com
- Material certificates/MTR: materialcert@fastenal.com, mtr@fastenal.com

Operational expectations:
- Fastenal expects suppliers to acknowledge and confirm all orders, ship exact products and quantities, follow PO pricing, ship from the closest possible location, ship on time, and keep open communication on issues.
- EDI is strategically important. Fastenal lists EDI 850 PO, 855 PO acknowledgment, 856 ASN with GS1-128 barcode label, 810 invoice, 846 inventory inquiry, 753 routing instruction for will-call suppliers, 820 remittance advice, 824 application advice, and 997 functional acknowledgment. Non-EDI supplier status is treated as temporary; product or inventory expansion can be held until the supplier becomes an EDI partner.
- Shelf-life products must arrive with adequate remaining shelf life: at least 85% remaining if expected shelf life is 12 months or less, and at least 75% if more than 12 months. Expiration/shelf-life must be on every packaging level.
- Cartons must not exceed 50 lb.
- All shippable/sellable units need approved barcode identification at piece/package/case levels. First-time product shipments may need sample barcode labels approved before shipment.
- Country of origin must be marked on all packaging levels and kept current in Fastenal's product files.
- Hazardous materials need proper class IDs, shipping-unit photos/packaging support when needed, GHS labels/SDS compliance, and certified hazmat shipping if using Fastenal UPS/FedEx accounts.
- Supplier quality expectations include a suitable QMS, document/record control, traceability, sub-tier supplier control, change notification, nonconforming product containment, corrective action, and environmental compliance documentation such as REACH/RoHS where applicable.

## Strategic Read

This should not be treated like a normal distributor form fill. Fastenal can accept a new supplier registration, but the commercial question is whether a store, account rep, category buyer, or customer has a reason to pull Glue Masters into the system.

Best path: create demand pull first, then register with a specific Fastenal store/contact where possible.

Glue Masters has a plausible fit because Fastenal already sells industrial adhesives and cyanoacrylate products. The wedge should be:
- Industrial CA adhesive for production/maintenance users.
- Fast cure, multiple viscosities, accelerator, bulk sizes.
- Domestic fulfillment from Chicago/US receiving reality.
- Price/value alternative to incumbent branded CA adhesive lines.
- Stronger for small/mid industrial accounts than commodity retail super glue.

## Readiness Gaps Before Submission

Must-have before supplier registration:
- W-9.
- Legal business name, address, ownership/contact details.
- Insurance info if requested.
- Product list with SKUs, sizes, GTIN/UPC/barcodes, case packs, weights/dimensions.
- SDS for every SKU.
- TDS for Thin/Medium/Thick/Accelerator.
- Country of origin per SKU and per packaging level.
- Shelf life / manufacture date / expiration date policy.
- Hazmat shipping classification per SKU/mode.
- Product photos and label photos.
- Wholesale price sheet and suggested Fastenal margin structure.
- Lead times, MOQ, case quantities, fulfillment location, inventory capacity.

Strongly recommended:
- CoA or batch test certificate format.
- Lot traceability SOP.
- Nonconforming product / recall / corrective action SOP.
- Barcode label examples for piece, pack, case.
- EDI readiness decision: SPS Commerce, existing 3PL EDI, or manual temporary path.
- 1-page Fastenal-specific sell sheet.

## 14-Day Action Plan

### Day 0-1: Build The Fastenal Packet

Owner: Ev / ops

Create a folder with:
- Fastenal sell sheet: product family, use cases, why it helps Fastenal customers.
- SKU matrix: item name, viscosity, size, case pack, UPC/GTIN, dimensions, weight, COO, shelf life, hazmat class, SDS/TDS links.
- Wholesale price ladder: case, 12-case, pallet, private/program pricing.
- Compliance docs: W-9, SDS, TDS, CoA template, lot traceability statement.
- Shipping SOP: carton weight under 50 lb, case labels, COO labels, expiration labels, hazmat/GHS markings.

### Day 1-3: Identify Fastenal Pull Points

Owner: sales

Target three paths in parallel:
- Local Chicago-area Fastenal store/rep for a branch-led onboarding path.
- Industrial account reps serving awards/personalized-goods, light manufacturing, woodworking, maintenance, and assembly shops.
- Category/procurement contact for adhesives/sealants/tape.

Goal: get one named Fastenal contact and a 5-letter store code before portal registration if possible.

Outreach angle:
- "We supply industrial CA adhesive in Thin/Medium/Thick plus accelerator, with bulk/customer production use cases. We want to become available to Fastenal branches/accounts where customers need reliable CA at better production economics. Who owns adhesive supplier onboarding or customer-driven adds?"

### Day 3-5: Register Supplier Portal

Owner: ops

Register as New/Potential Supplier at Fastenal.com:
- Use a monitored email inbox.
- Choose Fastenal Supplier and "not an existing Fastenal supplier."
- If a store contact/code was secured, enter it.
- If not, leave store field blank, but immediately follow with a concise email to suppliercompliance@fastenal.com referencing the product packet and asking for the correct buyer/category routing.

If approved:
- Save Supplier ID.
- Update the packet with Supplier ID.
- Ask for next steps for SKU activation and compliance review.

If denied:
- Reply asking what relationship, documentation, or buyer sponsorship is required for reconsideration.

### Day 5-10: Compliance + EDI Readiness

Owner: ops / 3PL

Do not wait for the first PO to solve this:
- Confirm whether ShipBob/current fulfillment can produce Fastenal-compliant labels, packing slips, COO/expiration labels, and case-level barcode labels.
- Decide EDI path. If volume is likely small at first, prepare for manual temporary fulfillment, but budget SPS Commerce/onboarding if Fastenal requests it.
- Prepare GS1/GTIN structure if not already clean.
- Confirm hazmat shipping class and limited quantity/ground transport status for each product with SDS support.
- Prepare package photos for hazmat/product compliance review.

### Day 7-14: Commercial Push

Owner: sales

Run targeted outbound to Fastenal-facing decision points:
- Branch managers near known Glue Masters industrial users.
- Account reps around top B2B target geographies.
- Category/procurement for adhesives.

Offer:
- Sample kit for internal evaluation.
- Customer trial with Fastenal rep attached.
- Intro pricing for first branch/customer order.
- Ability to support production-size bottles and replenishment cadence.

## First PO Target

Best first order if the goal is 16oz economics:
- 1-3 cases each of 16oz Medium and 16oz Thick, using the actual 30-unit case pack.
- 1 case 16oz Thin only if the account has wicking/penetrating applications.
- Accelerator as attach SKU, but not as the main economic driver.
- 2oz bottles should be used only as sample/trial units, not as the primary Fastenal SKU strategy.

Avoid leading with the full catalog. Get a limited, clean 16oz production-size activation first, then expand.

## 16oz Alignment Addendum

16oz aligns better with Fastenal than 2oz/8oz if the target is account-driven replenishment rather than retail-style shelf sales.

Why it fits:
- Fastenal's model is strongest with industrial customers, onsite accounts, vending/bin-stock, repeat purchasing, and maintenance/production replenishment.
- A 16oz CA bottle is a production/maintenance consumption SKU. That matches Fastenal's account model better than small hobby/retail bottles.
- Fastenal already carries larger industrial adhesive and chemical formats, including 1 lb / large-format products from incumbent brands. Glue Masters should position 16oz as the value/performance alternative for users consuming adhesive by the case.
- The commercial wedge is not "add another glue." It is "give branches and account reps a production-size CA option with better unit economics, reliable availability, and fast domestic replenishment."

What changes:
- The Fastenal packet should lead with 16oz Thin/Medium/Thick case packs, not 2oz/8oz.
- The sample kit can still include 2oz bottles because evaluators do not need a full 16oz bottle to test bond strength, viscosity, cure speed, and substrate fit.
- The first PO should be case-based around the real 30 x 16oz master case, then scale to multi-case and pallet economics.
- Pricing should be framed as cost per ounce and cost per bond versus Loctite/Permabond-style incumbents, not as a generic bottle price.
- Target Fastenal branches/accounts with production use: light manufacturing, awards/trophies, woodworking/millwork, MRO, fixture/display assembly, filter/speaker/component assembly, repair benches, and customers already buying CA in larger sizes.

Likely objection:
- Fastenal may not want to stock slow-moving 16oz branch inventory without account demand. Solve by starting as a customer-driven / non-stock / direct-ship SKU, then graduate to stocked inventory only after repeat orders.

Operational watchout:
- Confirm SDS transport language for each 16oz SKU. Similar industrial CA products are often manageable for domestic ground in non-bulk containers, but Fastenal will still expect accurate hazmat/GHS/product-compliance data and shelf-life labeling.

## Field Note: Branch Rep Reality

Fresh branch feedback changes the go-to-market approach:

- Vendors commonly blast line cards to branches, but that alone does not create mindshare.
- Loctite is memorable because they do educational events, bring food, and give reps a reason to stop and learn the product.
- Cost matters heavily if quality is on par with competitors.
- Adhesives are not a deep-focus category for many reps. Reps carry thousands of vendor/customer/SKU matches and are not specialists in every product line.

Implication: the Fastenal play should not be "send a line card and wait." It should be a **rep enablement wedge**:

1. Give reps a short comparison: Glue Masters 16oz CA vs incumbent CA adhesives on cost per ounce, use case, and quality parity.
2. Make the first ask easy: "When a customer asks for CA adhesive by the case, send us the application and we will help map viscosity/product."
3. Offer a short branch lunch/demo instead of a generic supplier pitch.
4. Give the branch something repeatable: a one-page cheat sheet, samples, and a simple customer-match script.
5. Use pricing as the hook, but avoid sounding cheap-only. The message is "quality on par, better production economics."

Revised local-branch objective:

Get one branch to accept a 20-minute educational visit/demo for industrial CA adhesive, with a simple line card and comparison sheet left behind for reps.

Suggested event positioning:

"We know reps are managing thousands of SKUs and adhesives are not always top of mind. We can do a short lunch-and-learn for the branch: when to use Thin/Medium/Thick CA, where 16oz case quantities make sense, and how Glue Masters compares on cost per ounce versus incumbent brands."

## Risks

- Blind supplier registration may be approved administratively but go nowhere commercially.
- Missing TDS/CoA/lot traceability can make the brand look too retail for Fastenal.
- Hazmat/GHS/barcode details can create receiving friction and penalties.
- EDI can become a scaling blocker even if first branch orders are manual.
- Fastenal already carries incumbent adhesive brands, so the pitch must be margin, availability, application fit, and customer demand, not "we sell glue too."

## Decision Recommendation

Proceed, but do it as a buyer/store-sponsored onboarding campaign, not a form-fill task.

The next concrete move is to build the Fastenal packet, then contact local/category Fastenal reps to secure a 5-letter store code or buyer sponsor before the portal submission. If no sponsor responds within 3 business days, submit the portal anyway and immediately email Supplier Compliance with the packet and category-routing request.
